Advanced Medical Life Support for Doctors

Duration
2 Days
Class Size
Up to 16 participants
CPD Credits
30 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)

Target Audience:

Particularly designed for medical practitioners who may be called upon to recognise and manage a patient with an acute or life-threatening medical emergency. A valid AHA BLS for Healthcare Provider Certificate is required.

Medical Emergencies Covered:

  • Breathing difficulties and respiratory conditions
  • Shock and haemorrhage
  • Chest pain and acute coronary syndromes
  • Altered consciousness and collapse
  • Overdose and toxicological emergencies
  • Headache and neurological conditions
  • Rashes and dermatological conditions
  • Abdominal pain and renal failure
  • Leg and joint conditions
  • Endocrine and metabolic conditions

Data Interpretation:

  • Chest X-ray evaluation
  • 12-lead ECG interpretation
  • Haematological, electrolyte, and blood gas analysis

Course Structure:

Hands-on practical training, tutorials, case discussions, role-plays, and data interpretation exercises.

Assessment and Preparation:

A 524-page textbook Acute Medical Emergencies – The Practical Approach and a 120-page handbook are supplied upon registration.

Certificate: Advanced Medical Life Support Certificate
