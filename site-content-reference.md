## Educational Philosophy & Accreditation

### Approach
Instruction follows "scientific evidence-based guidelines of the American Heart Association (AHA), the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the International Liaison Committee on Resuscitation (ILCOR)."

Lead Instructors are "internationally qualified and world leaders in emergency cardiovascular care."

The academy provides comprehensive study materials and internationally-recognised credentials upon successful course completion.

- **HPCSA** — Health Professions Council of South Africa (Continuing Professional Development / CPD credits)

## Courses

### 1. Basic Life Support for Healthcare Providers (BLS)
- **Duration:** 5 hours
- **Class size:** Up to 18 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 11 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Any healthcare worker who has a voluntary or professional duty to respond to a cardiac or respiratory emergency
- **Prerequisites:** None (AHA BLS Manual supplied prior to course). This certification is a mandatory prerequisite for ACLS, PALS, and ANLS.
- **What you learn:**
  - Initial CPR across all age groups
  - Fundamental airway techniques and rescue breathing
  - Bag-valve resuscitator operation
  - Automated External Defibrillation (AED)
  - Choking management for conscious/unconscious adults, children, and infants
  - Scene safety, standard precautions, spinal injury considerations
- **Teaching method:** "Relaxed, video-based practice-while-watching interactive adult-learning environment"
- **Certification:** American Heart Association BLS for Healthcare Providers Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/bls.jpg`

---

### 2. Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support Provider (ACLS)
- **Duration:** 2½ Days
- **Class size:** Up to 16 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including 2 CEUs for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Registered Medical or Dental Practitioner, Intern, Junior Intern, Professional Nurse, or Advanced Life Support Paramedic
- **Prerequisites:** Valid AHA BLS for Healthcare Provider Certificate. Pre-course study materials (ACLS Provider Manual + Algorithm Memory Cards) must be completed before attending.
- **What you learn:**
  - Recognising and managing cardiac arrest using systematic, algorithm-based approaches
  - Respiratory arrest management
  - Arrhythmia recognition and management
  - Ischaemic chest pain and acute coronary syndromes
  - Stroke care
  - Effective communication skills and leadership of a resuscitation team
- **Teaching method:** Practical, simulated case-based course with video demonstrations, instructor modelling, and hands-on practice. Participants lead multiple simulated clinical scenarios with constructive feedback.
- **Pre-course materials:** AHA ACLS Provider Manual and set of Algorithm Memory Cards (supplied)
- **Certification:** American Heart Association ACLS Card and Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/acls.jpg`

---

### 3. ACLS for Experienced Providers (ACLS-EP)
- **Duration:** 2 Days
- **Class size:** Up to 16 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including 2 CEUs for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Healthcare professionals with a current AHA ACLS Provider Certificate. Particularly suited to EMS, Emergency Department and ICU staff, and medical practitioners who regularly participate in resuscitation.
- **Prerequisites:** Current American Heart Association ACLS Provider Certificate
- **Topics covered:**
  - ACLS skills update
  - Advanced airway and oxygenation techniques
  - Circulation devices
  - Emergency cardiac pharmacology
  - Dangerous arrhythmias
  - Acute chest pain conditions and coronary syndromes
  - Electrolyte and acid-base disorders
  - Toxicology
  - Hypothermia and submersion incidents
  - Severe asthma and anaphylaxis
  - Pregnancy-related cardiac arrest
  - Shock management, pulmonary oedema, hypotension
  - Trauma and electrical injuries
  - Legal and ethical considerations
  - Post-resuscitation care
- **Teaching method:** "Intense practical and case-based" learning with discussion and hands-on practice, guided by positive and constructive feedback.
- **Pre-course materials:** 444-page AHA ACLS Resource Text (supplied before course)
- **Certification:** American Heart Association ACLS for Experienced Providers Card and Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/acls-ep.jpg`

---

### 4. Paediatric Advanced Life Support Provider (PALS)
- **Duration:** 2 Days
- **Class size:** Up to 16 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Registered Medical or Dental Practitioner, Intern, Junior Intern, Professional Nurse, or Advanced Life Support Paramedic
- **Prerequisites:** Valid AHA BLS for Healthcare Provider Certificate. Prior ACLS participation recommended but not required.
- **What you learn:**
  - Systematic assessment of seriously ill/injured children
  - Managing respiratory distress, respiratory failure, and shock states
  - Recognising life-threatening arrhythmias and arrest rhythms
  - Respiratory management including oxygen delivery and intubation
  - Electrical therapy (defibrillation and cardioversion)
  - IV and intra-osseous (IO) access
- **Teaching method:** "Practical, case-based Course" with extensive hands-on practice through simulated scenarios. Multiple team leadership opportunities with constructive feedback.
- **Pre-course materials:** AHA PALS Providers Manual, algorithm memory cards, practice cases, and pre-course self-assessment test (supplied)
- **Certification:** American Heart Association PALS Provider Certificate and Card
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/pals.jpg`

---

### 5. Paediatric ALS for Experienced Providers (PALS-EP)
- **Duration:** 2 Days
- **Class size:** Up to 12 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Senior healthcare professionals (Medical or Dental Practitioners, Professional Nurses, or Advanced Life Support Paramedics) with valid PALS provider certification who manage paediatric patients in acute emergency or ICU settings.
- **Prerequisites:** Valid PALS Provider Certificate + AHA BLS Provider Certificate
- **Day 1:** Practical, case-based learning with hands-on simulations
- **Day 2 advanced topics:** Burns, status epilepticus, management of DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis), trauma, and ventilation
- **What you learn:**
  - Systematic assessment of seriously ill/injured children
  - Managing respiratory distress and compensated and hypotensive shock
  - Recognising life-threatening arrhythmias and arrest rhythms
  - Respiratory management including oxygen delivery and intubation
  - Electrical therapy including defibrillation and cardioversion
  - IV and intra-osseous access techniques
- **Certification:** American Heart Association PALS Certificate + Academy of Advanced Life Support Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/pals-refresher.jpg`

---

### 6. Advanced Medical Life Support for Doctors (AMLS)
- **Duration:** 2 Days
- **Class size:** Up to 16 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Medical practitioners who may need to recognise and manage acute or life-threatening medical emergencies
- **Prerequisites:** AHA BLS for Healthcare Provider Certificate
- **Medical emergencies covered:**
  - Breathing difficulties
  - Shock
  - Chest pain
  - Altered consciousness and collapse
  - Overdose
  - Headache
  - Rashes
  - Abdominal pain
  - Renal failure
  - Leg and joint conditions
  - Respiratory, neurological, dermatological, endocrine, and metabolic conditions
- **Data interpretation / investigations:**
  - Chest X-ray evaluation
  - 12-lead ECG interpretation
  - Haematological, electrolyte, and blood gas analysis
- **Teaching method:** Hands-on practical training, tutorials, case discussions, role-plays, and data interpretation
- **Pre-course materials:** 524-page textbook "Acute Medical Emergencies – The Practical Approach" + 120-page handbook upon registration
- **Certification:** Advanced Medical Life Support Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/amls.jpg`

---

### 7. Emergency ECG and Pharmacology Course (ECG)
- **Duration:** 1 Day
- **Class size:** Up to 16 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 9 CEUs (HPCSA approved)
- **Target audience:** Healthcare professionals who need to recognise and interpret life-threatening cardiac rhythms and provide emergency interventions
- **Content modules:**
  - **Emergency Electrocardiography Module:** Foundational cardiac anatomy and electrophysiology. Systematic recognition and emergency management of bradycardias, tachycardias, supraventricular and ventricular arrhythmias, atrioventricular blocks, pacing, and synchronised cardioversion. Comprehensive 12-lead ECG interpretation including acute coronary syndromes and electrolyte disturbances.
  - **Clinical and Applied Pharmacology Module:** Vasopressors, anti-arrhythmics, rate control agents, and reperfusion therapies integrated with evidence-based algorithms.
- **Pre-course materials:** Pre-course text supplied for preparation
- **Certification:** Academy of Advanced Life Support Certificate + HPCSA approved CPD certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/ecg.jpg`

---

### 8. Advanced Neonatal Life Support Course (ANLS / NRP)
- **Duration:** 1 Day
- **Class size:** Up to 12 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 18 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Based on:** Evidence-based Neonatal Resuscitation Program by the American Academy of Pediatrics and American Heart Association
- **Target audience:** Healthcare professionals whose roles may involve managing or participating in newborn resuscitation
- **Prerequisites:** AHA BLS for Healthcare Provider Certificate
- **What you learn:**
  - Initial neonatal resuscitation steps
  - Proper use of ventilation devices
  - Chest compression techniques
  - Endotracheal intubation
  - Medication administration
  - Special birth considerations
  - Preterm infant care
  - Ethical issues and end-of-life care
- **Teaching method:** Practical, case-based program with video demonstrations, instructor guidance, and hands-on practice with simulated clinical scenarios
- **Pre-course materials:** 270-page American Academy of Pediatrics Textbook of Neonatal Resuscitation + Multimedia DVD-ROM (resuscitation footage, laryngoscopic imagery, interactive scenarios, 3-D animations)
- **Certification:** Resuscitation Council of Southern Africa and South African Paediatric Association Advanced Neonatal Life Support Certificate
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/nrp.jpg`

---

### 9. ACLS and PALS Update Course
- **Duration:** 1 Day + additional self-directed study
- **Class size:** Up to 8 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 30 CEUs (including CEUs for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Persons with a valid ACLS or PALS certificate that is about to expire who want to update their provider status
- **Prerequisites:** Current (near-expiring) AHA ACLS or PALS Provider Certificate + current edition of the relevant course manual
- **Format:** Pre-study materials (current provider manual + online assessments), then one-day in-person session covering skills review and practical evaluation
- **Certification:** Updated AHA Provider Certificate for the relevant course (ACLS or PALS)

---

### 10. International Trauma Life Support (ITLS)
- **Duration:** 2 Days
- **Class size:** Up to 8 participants
- **CPD Credits:** 23 CEUs (including 1 CEU for Ethics)
- **Target audience:** Any registered medical professional — doctors, nurses, and EMS staff expected to assess and manage acute trauma. Both basic and advanced versions offered.
- **Prerequisites:** None stated (manual supplied beforehand for preparation)
- **Pass requirement:** Written assessment minimum 74% + successful completion of a simulated scenario
- **Topics covered:**
  - Scene safety and triage
  - Trauma victim assessment and initial care
  - Airway management in trauma
  - Shock and haemorrhage control
  - Head and spinal trauma
  - Chest, abdominal, and extremity trauma
  - Burns
  - Trauma in pregnant patients, children, and elderly populations
  - Trauma cardiac arrest
- **Practical skill stations:**
  - Haemorrhage control techniques including tourniquets
  - IV/IO access
  - Tension pneumothorax decompression
  - Spinal motion restriction and patient extrication
  - Extremity stabilisation
- **Certification:** ITLS Certificate (international)
- **Image:** `assets/images/courses/itls.jpg`
